In the ancient Chinese medical book Treatise on Febrile Diseases, the littleblue dragon recipe of the small green dragon formula accidentally travels to thereal world and becomes a clumsy paper character. In order to find his identity,he leads medical students who are reading Treatise on Febrile Diseases into thewonderful world of books. From climbing mountains and collecting herbs, tostudying the ancient method of cooking the small green dragon soup, he guidesthem to explore the profound inner meaning of traditional Chinese medicineculture as shown in the medical book Treatise on Febrile Diseases.In this project, our team explored how animation and film can be effectivelycombined to create an engaging and educational piece with cultural significance.Through the interactions between the Little Blue Dragon character and theprotagonist, the film introduces audiences to the rich traditions of Chinesemedicine in an entertaining way. This project highlights the impact of interactiveand immersive media in cultural promotion, showcasing how these techniquescan deepen understanding and appreciation for traditional practices like those inChinese medicine.

Storyboard

01 - 05
The Recipe Wakes Up

We open in a grounded reality—reading, tools, and tabletop details anchor the medical text in the present. As ink ripples into a vortex, the scene pivots from live-action realism to magical logic: the “Little Blue Dragon” recipe begins to stir, signaling that the book is not only a source of knowledge, but a gateway with its own rules.

06 - 10
Into the Mountains

This sequence shifts into a quest structure. Wide landscapes establish the world; mid shots track the character’s movement; close-ups on the basket and herbs turn tradition into an actionable objective. By moving from macro to micro, the storyboard translates TCM knowledge into visible, playable steps—environment, path, and props all carry meaning.

11 - 17
Brewing the Decoction

We treat brewing as choreography. Overhead shots clarify structure and sequence; inserts and close-ups emphasize hands, tools, and liquid texture; a back-view anchors the viewer inside the ritual. Functionally, it explains the process—but it also prepares the visual grammar for the crossover, as live-action realism and animated cues begin to share the same space.

18 - 21
When Worlds Collide

The finale completes the crossover through a simple visual logic—drop, ripple, portal. The Little Blue Dragon’s impact triggers an expanding ink-and-water reaction, and the page transforms from medium to doorway. The closing tableau (tools, pot, open book) makes the theme explicit: the ancient text and the present-day learner now occupy one shared reality, launching the story into its book-world exploration.
